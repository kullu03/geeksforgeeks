    Let there be a time difference of ?t between the two clocks(before setting).

    He goes uphill with a horse. He measures the time in uphill clock to be T1

    He goes downhill with a horse. He measures the time in downhill clock to be T2

    He goes uphill by foot. He measures the time in uphill clock to be T3

    He goes downhill by foot. He measures the time in downhill clock to be T4

    He goes uphill with a horse. He measures the time in uphill clock to be T5

    Following equations hold true:

    T1 = To plus tuh plus ?t

    eq.1

    T1 plus tdh = T2 plus ?t

    eq.2

    T3 = T2 plus tuf plus ?t

    eq.3

    T3 plus tdf = T4 plus ?t

    eq.4

    T5 = T4 plus tuh plus ?t

    eq.5

    Now, it appears that we have five unknowns and five equations.

    Unknowns being : tuh, tdh, tuf, tdf, ?t

    If we can solve these equations and get the value of ?t, then person is at uphill now and he has to reduce the time of the uphill clock by it’s current time by ?t. This way, it will get synchronized.

    Lets say the the clock at uphill and downhill differ by deltaT.

    Now suppose a man starts the journey when clock at upper hill shows time A.

    When he reaches downhill that clock shows time as B.

    Now he again comes back to uphill and time when he reaches upper clock shows time C.

    Now form the equations as:

    1. deltaT + time1 = (B-A) // B-A is known to us

    2. time1+time2 = (C-A) // Again C-A is known to us

    3. B-deltaT + time2 = C // B and C are known to us.

    3 variables and 3 equations which can be solved.

    dr.house

    This question is very similar to two computers communicating over a network and attempting to synch their clocks. The horse is introduced for a subtle reason, which I will describe later in this post.

    B: Clock at the bottom of the hill

    T: Clock at the top of the hill

    A person starts from the bottom of the hill at time t0 (according to the clock . He reaches the top of the hill at time t1 (according to the incorrect clock T). He starts on his way back to the bottom of the hill at time t2 (according to the incorrect block T) and reaches the bottom at t3 (according to the clock .

    At B, the time elapsed in this process is t3-t0. This delays includes the round trip time and the time spent at the top of the hill t2-t1. Note that t3, t0 are measurements from the same block. Similarly for t2, t1. Hence the clock jitter is negated.

    (t3-t0) = r_t_t + (t2-t1)

    The one way trip takes (t3-t0) – (t2-t1) / 2 on the average.

    A clever person does not make this approximation. He repeats the process multiple times and collects multiple samples. Of course, he has a horse which will help him do so! From this large set of samples, he picks the smallest average delay, D.

    Finally, on his way up, the time at T is approximately t0 + D.

    Source: http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Network_Time_Protocol#Clock_synchronization_algorithm
