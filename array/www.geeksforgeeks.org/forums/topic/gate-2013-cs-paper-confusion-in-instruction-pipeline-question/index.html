    Hello Geeks,

    This is regarding to my confusion related to Instruction Pipeline Question which was asked in GATE 2013 CS paper. Question is as following:

    —

    Consider an instruction pipeline with five stages without any branch prediction: Fetch Instruction
    (FI), Decode Instruction (DI), Fetch Operand (FO), Execute Instruction (EI) and Write Operand
    (WO). The stage delays for FI, DI, FO, EI and WO are 5 ns, 7 ns, 10 ns, 8 ns and 6 ns, respectively.
    There are intermediate storage buffers after each stage and the delay of each buffer is 1 ns. A
    program consisting of 12 instructions I1, I2, I3, â€¦, I12 is executed in this pipelined processor.
    Instruction I4 is the only branch instruction and its branch target is I9. If the branch is taken during
    the execution of this program, the time (in ns) needed to complete the program is

    (A) 132 (B) 165 (C) 176 (D) 328

    —

    GATE officials have mentioned <(B) 165> as the correct answer choice in recently uploaded key. Although, it doesn’t seem the correct answer, as I’m getting it as 133 according to following specified method, which is very close to the answer choice <(A) 132>.

    Following is what I had attempted during exam.

    Notes:
    (1) bd = buffer delay
    (2) Cell Value represents the TIME at which, particular INSTRUCTION would finish its work in specific STAGE.

     

    # FI bd DI bd FO bd EI bd WO
    I1 5 6 13 14 24 25 33 34 40
    I2 10 11 20 21 34 35 43 44 50
    I3 15 16 27 28 44 45 53 54 60
    I4 20 21 34 35 54 55 63 64 70
    I9 68 69 76 77 87 88 96 97 103
    I10 73 74 83 84 97 98 106 107 113
    I11 78 79 90 91 107 108 116 117 123
    I12 83 84 97 98 117 118 126 127 133

     

    What do you think? What should be the correct answer? Please comment your answers with explanations if any.
