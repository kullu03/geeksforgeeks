    Hi,

    I was asked this very recently of how to find a median in BST? I wanted to know what are the different approaches we have. One way I could think of is traverse inorder and return the middle element. This takes O(n) for traversal, O(n) extra space and O(1) for finding the median. Is there a better approach?

  • May 3, 2010 at 5:37 PM #92088

    geek4u
    Participant

    If BST is balanced such that there are always half nodes on left side of the root and half on right side then we can guarantee O(1) and time and space complexity for median.

    Example trees

    Example of a complete binary tree


    
4
    
/       
    
2            6
    
/             /    
    
1      3       5     7

    Even if the tree is not complete but balanced, then we can find in O(1) by storing count of nodes in subtree formed by the node as a tree. 


    
4 (7)
    
/           
    
2(2)            6 (3)
    
/                   /    
    
1(0)  3(0)   5(0)  7(1)
    

    
8(0)

    Traverse the list from root in Inorder and reverse Inorder simultaneously. The two traversal will meet the median node. This way we are traversing tree once only.
