    I think as pointed out by Pradeey, there are many variations to this question. Sticking to the requirement of the question stated here, if we only need to measure 15 minutes starting from now, the thing that we need to ensure is that the stick keeps burning at 4 (and exactly 4) different points at all times until the stick is fully burnt out. The reason for this is – Since the stick is not uniform -but- we know the fact that it will completely burn out in exactly 1 hour (by burning from one end only), the various time units can be measured by (1) increasing the number of points on the stick, and/or (2) lighting the stick at different intervals of time.

    So, refering back to the original question of 15 mins, if we have 4 active burning points on the stick (say, to start with we can have 2 extreme ends + center of the stick) for the entire life of the stick, that would ensure that the 60 mins is divided by 4 implying 15 mins.

    Also, note that when we light the stick at the center, it will start burning both, to the left and to the right from there.

    i don’t think there exists a method to exactly measure 15 mins by the given constraints;-

    ->the stick in non-uniform

    ->only one stick is allowed

    one of these conditions need to be eliminated for measuring the time…

    a recursive solution could be as follows…

    We are given a rope of 1-hour burn time and we need to measure 15 minutes. Doubling the speed is easy by burning the rope from both the ends but the same is not true when you need to increase the speed four fold.

    For example, if you cut a rope into two halves H1 and H2 with burning time 10 and 50 respectively. Now, burning both of them from both the ends does not help as H1 will burn in 5 minutes where as H2 will take 25 minutes.

    Anyways, the solution is , you cut the rope into two pieces ( need not be half, because of non-uniformity it does not matter whether you cut at the middle point or not ) P1 and P2. Note that we need to measure 15 minute with 60-minutes burn time rope. Or in short, given a rope of length L( in time when burned from one end ) we need to measure L/4.

    Burn, P1 and P2 from both the ends. P1 = 30-x and P2 = 30+x where x is in [0,30). Because we are burning from both the ends, P1 will get burnt in 15-(x/2) minutes. For P2 , 30+x – 2*(15-(x/2)) = 2x, because P2 is also burning from both the ends. At the end of 15-(x/2) minutes P2 will have length 2x remaining. To measure exactly 15 minutes we need to measure time x/2 after 15-(x/2) has elapsed.

    We are again back to the same problem. We have a rope of length 2x and we need to measure x/2 which is increasing the speed four fold. So, we can keep on repeating this experiment. How long do we need to repeat this? Well, until both the pieces finish burning exactly at the same time. Why?

    Say we have a rope of length y and we need to measure y/4. Two pieces P1 and P2 measures y-z and z. When both are lighted from both the ends time taken will be (y-z)/2 and z/2 respectively.

    If they finish burning at the same time then

    (y-z)/2 = z/2

    => y = 2z

    Burning time was z/2 = y/4 which is what we wanted.
