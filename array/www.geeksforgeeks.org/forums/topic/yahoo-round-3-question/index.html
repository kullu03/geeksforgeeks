    Given an array of length N. How will you find the minimum length contiguous sub – array of whose sum is S and whose product is P . Here S and P will be given to you.

  • October 19, 2010 at 8:04 AM #101718

    Anonymous

    A simple method would be to use two loops where the outer loop picks elements one by one from left right and checks for sum and product of the sub-array starting from the picked element.

    Time Complexity of this solution is O(n^2)

  • October 19, 2010 at 8:30 AM #101719

    Anonymous

    If all numbers are positive then it can be done in O(n).

    Let the input array be arr[], sum be S and product be P. First locate the given S and then match P. If P matches then we have a match, otherwise locate the next S.

    Keep track of starting and ending positions of sub-starting. Let the positions be s and e respectively.

    Initialize s = 0, e = 0 and sum = arr, product = arr

    If sum < S, then e++, sum = sum + arr[e], product = product*arr[e].

    If sum > S then sum = sum – arr, product = product/arr, s++,

    If sum == S and product == P then print s and e.

    If sum == S and product > P then sum = sum – arr, product = product/arr, s++

    If sum == S and product < P then e++, sum = sum + arr[e], product = product*arr[e].
